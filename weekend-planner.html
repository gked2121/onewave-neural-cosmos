Day 1 — Saturday, March 21

Drive down, launch jet ski, sunset vibes
7:00 AM
Hit the Road
Load up the jet ski trailer, grab coffee, and head south on the Turnpike → US-1. Enjoy the iconic Overseas Highway drive across the bridges.
9:30 AM
Arrive & Check In
Drop bags at your Airbnb. Most places allow early drop-off by 10am. Unload and get settled.
10:30 AM
🚤 Launch the Jet Ski
Head to Harry Harris Park (MM 92.5). Launch fee is $25 non-resident each way. Ride out to the turquoise flats — explore shallow sandbars, hit Indian Key, and cruise the bayside channels.
1:00 PM
Lunch at Lazy Days
Oceanfront dining with fresh-catch fish tacos and cold drinks. Right on the water in Islamorada.
2:30 PM
🛶 Kayak the Mangroves
Paddle through the iconic mangrove tunnels. Crystal-clear shallow water, spotted eagle rays, small sharks, and total serenity. Rent kayaks locally or bring your own.
5:00 PM
Robbie's Marina — Tarpon Feeding
Stop at the legendary Robbie's to hand-feed massive tarpon off the dock. Grab a drink at the Hungry Tarpon bar. Great photo ops.
6:30 PM
🍽️ Dinner at Morada Bay Beach Café
Tables in the sand, tiki torches, live music. One of the most romantic restaurants in the Keys. Fresh seafood and craft cocktails as the sun goes down.
8:30 PM
Stargazing & Nightcap
The Keys have some of the darkest skies in South Florida. Grab a drink and walk the beach or relax on your Airbnb deck.
📅

Day 2 — Sunday, March 22

Snorkel the reef, brunch, head home
8:00 AM
Sunrise Paddle
Early morning kayak while the water is glass-calm. Best conditions for spotting stingrays, nurse sharks, and sea turtles in the shallows.
9:30 AM
Brunch at Fish House Encore
Famous Key Largo spot. Try the lobster Benedict or the hogfish — locally caught, absolutely incredible.
11:00 AM
🤿 Snorkel the Coral Reef
Book a Fury Cat or John Pennekamp tour to snorkel North America's only living coral reef. Incredible visibility, tropical fish, and maybe a sea turtle sighting.
1:30 PM
Last Jet Ski Ride & Pull Out
One more rip across the flats before hauling out. Take the scenic route past the sandbars.
3:00 PM
Pack Up & Head Home
Scenic drive back up US-1. Should arrive in Coconut Creek by ~5:30 PM with time to unload and rest up for the week.

Day 1 — Saturday, March 21

Gulf Coast jet skiing & mangrove exploration
7:30 AM
Head West on Alligator Alley
Take I-75 across the Everglades — keep an eye out for gators along the canal. Quick, easy 2-hour drive.
9:30 AM
Arrive & Check In
Drop bags at your Airbnb in Naples or Marco Island. Get oriented and unload the trailer.
10:30 AM
🚤 Launch at Cocohatchee River Park
4 lanes, 60 trailer spots, courtesy docks — the best launch in the area. Ride out to the Gulf. Calm, warm waters with incredible visibility.
1:00 PM
Lunch on Marco Island
Fresh grouper sandwiches and cold beers at a waterfront spot. Walk the pristine white-sand beach after.
2:30 PM
🐚 Shelling on Marco Island
Tigertail Beach has some of the best shelling on the Gulf Coast. Find sand dollars, lightning whelks, and fighting conchs.
4:30 PM
🛶 Guided Mangrove Eco-Tour
Rising Tide Explorers offers biologist-led kayak tours through Marco's mangroves. Spot dolphins, manatees, and osprey nests.
6:30 PM
🌅 Sunset at Naples Pier
The iconic Naples Pier at golden hour — one of the best sunsets in Florida. Dolphins often play in the surf right at the pier.
7:30 PM
🍽️ Date Night Dinner
Naples has upscale dining — try a Gulf-to-table spot on Fifth Avenue South. Dress up a bit; it's worth it.
📅

Day 2 — Sunday, March 22

Rookery Bay, Ten Thousand Islands, head home
7:30 AM
🛶 Rookery Bay Reserve
110,000 acres of pristine mangrove estuary. Launch early for glassy water and wildlife sightings — one of the most untouched ecosystems in Florida.
10:00 AM
Brunch in Naples
Fuel up at a café in the charming downtown Naples area. Açaí bowls, eggs Benedict, mimosas.
11:30 AM
🚤 Final Jet Ski Session
One more ride on the Gulf. Explore the Ten Thousand Islands if you're feeling adventurous — a maze of mangrove islands that feels like another planet.
2:00 PM
Pack Up & Drive Home
Back across Alligator Alley. Home by ~4:00 PM with plenty of time to rest up.
